India Gate, located in the heart of New Delhi, is a war memorial dedicated to the 84,000 Indian soldiers who died during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and inaugurated in 1931, the 42-meter-high arch stands as a symbol of national pride and valor. Constructed in sandstone, India Gate also houses the Amar Jawan Jyoti, the eternal flame honoring unknown soldiers, added after the 1971 Indo-Pak war. Surrounded by lush green lawns, it is a popular spot for evening strolls, patriotic celebrations, and cultural events. At night, the structure is beautifully lit, making it a breathtaking sight and one of Delhi's most visited monuments.
