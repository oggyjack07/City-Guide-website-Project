Red Fort - The Iconic Mughal Fortress

The Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an iconic 17th-century fortress built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan when he shifted his capital from Agra to Delhi. Constructed from red sandstone that gives it its name, this massive complex covers 254 acres and is surrounded by 2.5 km of defensive walls. The fort's impressive architecture includes the Lahore Gate (main entrance), the Chatta Chowk (covered bazaar), the Diwan-i-Am (Hall of Public Audience), and the Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience) where the famous Peacock Throne once stood. Every year on Independence Day, the Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag at the Red Fort and addresses the nation. The fort's intricate marble inlays, beautiful gardens, and blend of Persian, Timurid, and Hindu architectural styles make it one of India's most visited monuments.
