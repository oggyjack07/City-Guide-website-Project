The Gateway of India is a grand arch-monument built during the 20th century in Mumbai. Located on the waterfront in the Apollo Bunder area in South Mumbai, it was built to commemorate the landing of King George V and Queen Mary in 1911. Designed in Indo-Saracenic style, the structure was completed in 1924 and stands 26 meters tall. Today, it is one of Mumbai‚Äôs most visited tourist attractions and serves as a departure point for ferries to Elephanta Caves. At sunset, the view of the Arabian Sea and this historical arch is simply breathtaking.
